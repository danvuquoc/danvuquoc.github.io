Power Assisted Steering (PAS) - Bleed - V6

Print

Operation Code: 32.03.20-00

    Power Assisted Steering Bleeding

  1. Raise and support vehicle. Refer to procedure.
  2. Check power steering fluid reservoir level and top up if necessary. Refer to technical data.
  3. With the engine NOT running, turn steering wheel from lock-to-lock.
  4. Check power steering fluid reservoir level and top up if necessary.
  5. Once power steering fluid reservoir level remains static, start engine and continue turning steering wheel from lock-to-lock.

    6. CAUTION: Do not hold steering on full lock as this will cause damage to the pump.

  6. Check power steering fluid reservoir level and top up if necessary.
  7. Once steering action feels smooth and consistent, and power steering fluid reservoir level remains static, lower vehicle and turn steering again from lock-to-lock to see if it remains smooth and consistent under load.

    8. NOTE: Vehicle ramps or polished painted workshop floors offer little resistance. If necessary position vehicle on a normal road surface to determine if steering action is acceptable.

  8. If the steering action is acceptable, check power steering fluid reservoir level and top up if necessary. No further action is required.

    Power Assisted Steering Pressure Bleeding

  1. Using hand vacuum pump MITYVAC MV8500, install modified cap to power steering fluid reservoir and secure other end of hose to liquid/vapour separator tank. Use second hose to connect liquid/vapour separator tank to vacuum pump.

    2. NOTE: Ensure liquid/vapour separator tank is higher than the power steering fluid reservoir.

  2. With vacuum pump securely connected, apply a maximum vacuum of -65 kPa / 20-25 inHg.

    3. NOTE: Observe vacuum level for 1 minute. If level drops, this indicates there is an air leak. Investigate and rectify source of leak.

  3. With all wheels on ground and vacuum applied, start vehicle and slowly turn steering from lock-to-lock for approximately 10-15 cycles.

    4. CAUTION: Do not hold steering on full lock as this will cause damage to the pump.

  4. Check power steering fluid reservoir level and top up if necessary.
  5. Release vacuum, remove modified cap from power steering fluid reservoir and replace original reservoir cap.
  6. If intermittent steering is still present after vacuum bleeding, position vehicle on a normal road surface, increase engine speed to 1250 rpm to raise power steering pump flow to maximum and turn steering wheel from lock-to-lock.
