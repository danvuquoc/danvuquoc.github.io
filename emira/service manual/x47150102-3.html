Driveshaft Assembly - Left Side - V6

Print

Operation Code: 47.15.01-02

    Removal

  1. Remove rear undertray. Refer to procedure.
  2. Remove left side rear hub carrier. Refer to procedure.
  3. Disconnect electrical connector from gearbox.
  4. Remove oil sensor and drain gearbox oil.
  5. Release driveshaft from gearbox.

    6. CAUTION: Take care not to damage dust cover ring on the driveshaft.

    CAUTION: Take care not to damage the gearbox oil seal to prevent leaks.

    NOTE: Use a suitable container to collect any gearbox oil spillage.

  6. Remove driveshaft from vehicle.

    7. Do not carry out further disassembly if removing for access only.

  7. Remove clips (x2) securing inboard constant velocity (CV) joint gaiter to driveshaft and remove or pull away gaiter.

    8. CAUTION: Take care not to damage the CV gaiter if it is to be reused.

  8. Match mark driveshaft and CV joint outer body.
  9. Remove CV joint outer body.
  10. Match mark driveshaft and CV joint spider.
  11. Remove snap ring from end of driveshaft and remove CV joint spider.
  12. Remove clips (x2) securing CV joint gaiter to driveshaft and remove or pull gaiter away from CV joint.
  13. Clean CV joint spider and degrease using suitable solvent.

    14. NOTE: Inspect condition of grease for contamination before cleaning.

    NOTE: Inspect the spider bearings for any significant wear within the driving surface. Discard and replace if necessary.

    Installation

  1. Installation is the reverse of removal procedure except for the following:
  2. Pack CV joint spider with grease.

    3. NOTE: Use supplied grease type NKG302.

    NOTE: Use all grease suppled for the respective joint.

  3. Apply grease to inboard driveshaft splines before installation.

    4. CAUTION: Take care when installing driveshaft into gearbox to not damage the snap ring.

    NOTE: Applying grease will keep the snap ring in place, aiding in installation.

  4. Release hose clamp securing gearbox oil cooler hose to gearbox fill point.
  5. Remove hose and allow oil to drain.
  6. Use Lotus Insight tool to actuate oil cooler pump until no more oil flows.
  7. Leave system undisturbed for 5 minutes.
  8. Refill transmission oil.

    9. NOTE: The transmission oil is full when oil begins to flow from the fill point.

  9. Check manual transmission oil level. Refer to procedure.
  10. Check automatic transmission oil level. Refer to procedure.
