AC System - Depressurise, Evacuate And Recharge System - V6

Print

Operation Code: 40.23.02-02

Important Information

WARNING: On no account should refrigerant ever be discharged to atmosphere - use a refrigerant recovery/recycling station in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions.

WARNING: Heavy concentrations of refrigerant vapour can produce toxic gas if exposed to a naked flame. The gas can also attack metal.

WARNING: Refrigerant is a hazardous liquid and if handled incorrectly can cause serious injury. Suitable protective clothing, consisting of face protection, heat-proof gloves, rubber boots and apron or waterproof overalls, must be worn when carrying out operations on the air conditioning system.

WARNING: Servicing must only be carried out by personnel familiar with both the vehicle system and the charging and testing equipment. All operations must be carried out in a well-ventilated area away from open flame and heat sources.

    Discharge

  1. Remove right side front wheel arch liner. Refer to procedure.

    2. NOTE: Procedure is the same as left side component.

  2. Remove cap secured to refrigerant gas outlet port.
  3. Connect air-conditioning discharge line to outlet port.
  4. Remove air conditioning refrigerant gas and depressurize system.

    Recharge

  1. Open all doors on the vehicle to allow fresh air to circulate through vehicle during the recharge process.

    2. WARNING: All doors must be open to prevent a build up of gas in the cabin in the event of a HVAC leak.

  2. Remove cap secured to air conditioning inlet port.
  3. Start and operate engine.
  4. Set air-conditioning to coldest setting.
  5. Connect air-conditioning recharge line to inlet port.
  6. Recharge / pressurize air-conditioning system.

    7. NOTE: Check inside the cabin to ensure air is cold.

  7. Refit AC charge port caps to discharge and recharge ports.
