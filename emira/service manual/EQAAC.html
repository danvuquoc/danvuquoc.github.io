VEHICLE IDENTIFICATION AND REPAIRS

Repairs and Replacements

When replacement parts are required, it is essential that only Lotus recommended parts are used. Attention is particularly drawn to the following points concerning repairs and the fitting of replacement parts and accessories. Safety features and corrosion prevention treatments embodied in the car may be impaired if other than Lotus recommended parts are fitted. In certain territories, legislation prohibits the fitting of parts not to the manufacturer's specification. Torque wrench setting figures given in this Manual must be used. Locking type fixings, where specified, must be fitted. If a locking fixing is disturbed or removed during a procedure then its efficiency is impaired and it must be renewed. Owners purchasing accessories while travelling abroad should ensure that the accessory and its fitted location on the car conform to legal requirements. The Terms of the vehicle Warranty may be invalidated by the fitting of other than Lotus recommended parts.All Lotus recommended parts have the full backing of the vehicle Warranty. Lotus Dealers are obliged to supply only Lotus recommended parts.

Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)

The Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) is a unique 17-digit number used to identify the car for licensing, warranty, spares ordering and administrative purposes.

The VIN comprises 17 characters, coded in accordance with European Economic Community (EEC) directives, and with those of the National Highway Traffic Safety Association (NHTSA). For change point identification in Service Notes, Service Bulletins and Service Parts Lists, typically, only characters 10 (model year), and 14 to 17 (serial number) will be quoted. For example 'N 1234' being 2022 model year, serial number 1234.

Note that the serial number sequence re-starts at 0001 for each model year, and is shared with all other Lotus models.

The full VIN should always be quoted with any vehicle enquiries, together with the engine number if the query is engine related. Factory records are filed against VIN and specification change points are identified by VIN or engine number.

VIN or engine number can be found in the following locations on the vehicle.

Central Display

From the centre display side bar, select > Global Settings and App Options > General > Car Information.

Windscreen

Viewable from the outside of the vehicle through the windscreen on the left hand front edge of the dashboard.

Chassis

Stamped on the cross member beneath the driver’s seat, accessible inside the cabin with the seat slid fully rearwards.

Fuel Tank Bay

Printed label stuck to the floor below the front edge of the right-hand rear or luggage bay. Pull back a flap in the carpet to view.
