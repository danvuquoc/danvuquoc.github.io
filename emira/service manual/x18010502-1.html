Expansion Valve

Print

Operation Code: 18.01.05-02

    Removal

  1. Depressurise air conditioning system. Refer to procedure.
  2. Remove pollen filter. Refer to procedure.
  3. Remove M6x16 bolt securing air conditioning pipe clamp plate to expansion valve. Torque 9 Nm.
  4. Remove air conditioning clamp plate.
  5. Remove M6x20 bolt from air conditioning pipe. Torque 10 Nm.
  6. Disconnect air conditioning pipe from fitting.

    7. NOTE: Cap pipe and fitting to prevent ingress of dirt and moisture.

    NOTE: Discard O ring.

  7. Disconnect air conditioning pipe from expansion valve.

    8. NOTE: Cap pipe and expansion valve port to prevent ingress of dirt and moisture.

    NOTE: Discard O ring.

  8. Remove M6x20 bolts (x2) securing air conditioning pipe to receiver dryer and front subframe. Torque 10 Nm.
  9. Release clip from air conditioning pipe.
  10. Disconnect air conditioning pipe from receiver dryer.

    11. NOTE: Cap pipe and receiver dryer port to prevent ingress of dirt and moisture.

    NOTE: Discard O ring.

  11. Disconnect air conditioning pipe from expansion valve.

    12. NOTE: Cap pipe and expansion valve port to prevent ingress of dirt and moisture.

    NOTE: Discard O ring.

  12. Remove allen bolts (x2) securing expansion valve to evaporator pipes. Torque 9 Nm.
  13. Remove expansion valve.

    14. NOTE: Cap pipes and expansion valve ports to prevent ingress of dirt and moisture.

    NOTE: Discard O rings.

    Installation

  1. Installation is the reverse of removal procedure except for the following:
  2. Renew discarded O rings.
  3. With AC compressor fitted, perform the following running in procedure:

    4. WARNING: The running in procedure described below must be performed so that refrigerant oil does not exit the compressor sump and flow directly into the air conditioning hoses/pipes before first circulating around the compressor, this ensures the compressors internal components are fully lubricated.

  4. Start engine.
  5. Turn on air conditioning to maximum cold setting.
  6. Allow engine to idle for 2 minutes while monitoring performance.
  7. Check for any leaks of AC refrigerant or oil.
