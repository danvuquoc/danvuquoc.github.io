Labour Operation Code Structure

To rationalise the information contained within the service notes, the removal, installation and/or replacement procedure information contained within each section has been divided into specific assembly areas or ‘function groups’ of the vehicle.

Every labour operation code consists of an 8-digit number, digit numbers 1 - 6 are the function code, digit numbers 7 - 8 are the work code.

Example:

31.01.06-02 Wheel Bearing Assembly - Front - Left Side

Digits 1 & 2 of the function code refer to the major function group, such as suspension:

31 - Suspension

Digits 3 & 4 refer to a general component, such as front suspension:

01 - Front Suspension

Digits 5 & 6 refer to a specific part, such as wheel bearing assembly:

06 - Wheel Bearing Assembly - Front - Left Side

Digits 7 & 8 are the work code:-00 Test/adjust-01 Remove and Refit (R & R)-02 Renew-03 Renew including paint-04 Overhaul-05 Renew - unit out of car-06 Retrofit-07 Modify

Selecting the operation code number link underneath the component description at the top of the 1st page of the procedure will direct you to the relevant labour time information for all components associated with that function group.

Unless otherwise stated the operation codes listed are ‘stand alone’ times. Only where reference is made to another operation code, should multiple operation times be claimed.

If the particular part to be claimed for is not illustrated, or if an operation code is not listed, then please contact the warranty department for prior authorisation. Please see the criteria for pre-authorisation as shown in the Lotus Cars warranty policy and procedures manual.

Diagnosis and testing

As a general rule, diagnosis and testing both before and after an operation should be considered an operating expense and cannot be claimed for under warranty unless prior authority has been granted.

General Use

Step by step component removal information comprising of illustrations and text is first displayed.

Any fixing or component being referred to within the text is colour highlighted within its accompanying illustration, arrows indicating the component may also be included.

As applicable, operations include reference to service tool numbers and an associated illustration to depict the tool. Where usage is not obvious, the tool is shown in use. Adjustment and repair operations also include reference to wear limits, relevant data, torque figures, and specialist information and useful assembly details

Some removal/installation procedures for components shown within a procedure may have already been described within another section of the service notes. When this occurs, a link is shown and when selected you will be directed to that procedure.

Installation information is shown after the removal procedure and is described as ‘Installation is the reverse of removal procedure’ unless additional procedures are necessary.

WARNINGS, CAUTIONS and NOTES have the following meanings:

WARNING: Procedures which must be followed precisely to avoid the possibility of injury.

CAUTION: Calls attention to procedures which must be followed to avoid damage to components.

NOTE: Gives helpful information.

References

Where a component is fitted to both the left and right side of the vehicle, the left side removal and installation procedure will be documented, with references to the right side noted if necessary.

References to the left side or right side described in the service notes is made when viewing the vehicle from the rear. With the engine and gearbox assembly removed, the crankshaft pulley end of the engine is referred to as the front.

Operations covered in this Manual do not include reference to testing the vehicle after repair. It is essential that work is inspected and tested after completion and if necessary, a road test of the vehicle is carried out, particularly where safety related items are concerned.
