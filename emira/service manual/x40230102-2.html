Air Conditioning Compressor - 4 Cylinder

Print

Operation Code: 40.23.01-02

    Removal

  1. Recover Air Conditioning (AC) refrigerant. Refer to procedure.
  2. Release tension from auxiliary drive belt and remove from AC compressor pulley. Refer to procedure.
  3. Remove rear bulkhead access panel. Refer to procedure.
  4. Disconnect harness connector (1) from AC compressor (A9).
  5. Remove bolts (2) (x2) securing AC refrigerant lines (3) to AC compressor. Torque 20 Nm.
  6. Disconnect AC refrigerant lines from AC compressor.

    7. NOTE: Plug exposed connections to prevent ingress of dirt.

    NOTE: Discard O-rings.

  7. Remove bolts (4) (x3) securing AC compressor to engine. Torque 20 Nm.
  8. Remove AC compressor.

    9. NOTE: If fitting new compressor, retain old compressor to measure oil quantity measurement.

    Installation

  1. Installation is the reverse of removal procedure except for the following:
  2. Drain and measure AC compressor oil quantity.

    3. NOTE: If the existing compressor is to be refitted after normal refrigerant recovery has been performed, a quantity of oil equivalent to that recovered must be added to that already held in the compressor.

    NOTE: If the system has suffered a rapid discharge, most of the refrigerant oil will have been lost. Drain the remaining oil from the compressor. Refill with 130g of oil before installation.

  3. Renew O-rings with new refrigerant oil.
  4. Recharge AC refrigerant.
  5. With AC compressor fitted, perform the following running in procedure:

    6. WARNING: The running in procedure described below must be performed so that refrigerant oil does not exit the compressor sump and flow directly into the air conditioning hoses/pipes before first circulating around the compressor, this ensures the compressors internal components are fully lubricated.

  6. Start engine.
  7. Turn on air conditioning to maximum cold setting.
  8. Allow engine to idle for 2 minutes while monitoring performance.
  9. Check for any leaks of AC refrigerant or oil.
