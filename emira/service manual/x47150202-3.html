Driveshaft Assembly - Right Side - V6

Print

Operation Code: 47.15.02-02

    Removal

  1. Remove downpipe. Refer to procedure.
  2. Remove hub carrier right side. Refer to procedure.

    3. NOTE: Procedure is the same as for left side component.

  3. Disconnect electrical connector from gearbox.
  4. Remove oil sensor and drain gearbox oil.
  5. Remove M10x50 mm bolts and nuts (x2) securing driveshaft to bearing carrier. Torque 45 Nm.
  6. Release driveshaft from bearing carrier.

    7. NOTE: Be prepared to catch any excess fluid.

  7. Remove driveshaft.

    8. Do not carry out further disassembly if remove for access only.

  8. Remove clips (x2) securing inboard constant velocity (CV) joint gaiter to driveshaft and remove or pull away gaiter.
  9. Match mark driveshaft and CV joint outer body.
  10. Remove CV joint outer body.
  11. Match mark driveshaft and CV joint spider.
  12. Remove snap ring from end of driveshaft and remove CV joint spider.
  13. Remove clips (x2) securing CV joint gaiter to driveshaft and remove or pull gaiter away from CV joint.
  14. Clean CV joint spider and degrease using suitable solvent.

    15. NOTE: Inspect condition of grease for contamination before cleaning.

    NOTE: Inspect the spider bearings for any significant wear within the driving surface. Discard and replace if necessary.

  15. Use press to remove dust shield from inboard end of driveshaft.
  16. Remove circlip from outboard face of bearing housing.

    17. NOTE: Discard and replace circlip.

  17. Use press to remove outboard bearing housing.

    Installation

  1. Installation is the reverse of removal procedure except for the following:
  2. Press new bearing into housing.
  3. Use special tool T000T1438F to press bearing case on to shoulder of driveshaft.
  4. Use special tool T000T1439F to press bearing dust shield on to shaft.
  5. Use press to fit inboard dust shield on to end of driveshaft the correct dimension.
  6. Release hose clamp securing gearbox oil cooler hose to gearbox fill point.
  7. Remove hose and allow oil to drain.
  8. Use Lotus insight tool to actuate oil cooler pump until no more oil flows.
  9. Leave system undisturbed for 5 minutes.
  10. Refill transmission oil.

    11. NOTE: The transmission oil is full when oil begins to flow from the fill point.

  11. Check manual transmission oil level. Refer to procedure.
  12. Check automatic transmission oil level. Refer to procedure.
