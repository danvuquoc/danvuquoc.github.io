Driveshaft Assembly - Left Side - 4 Cylinder

Print

Operation Code: 47.15.01-02

Important Information

WARNING: Transmission oil is a toxic substance and can be lethal if ingested. Wear protective gloves, protective clothing and safety glasses.

    Removal

  1. Remove rear undertray. Refer to procedure.
  2. Remove left side rear hub carrier. Refer to procedure.
  3. Release driveshaft from transmission.

    4. CAUTION: Take care not to damage the transmission oil seal to prevent leaks.

    NOTE: Use a suitable container to collect any transmission oil.

  4. Remove driveshaft.

    5. Do not carry out further disassembly if removing for access only.

  5. Using a suitable tool, release clamps (2 and 4) and slide gaiter (3) along driveshaft (1).

    6. CAUTION: Take care not to damage the gaiter if it is to be reused.

  6. Release gaiter seal (5) from joint hub (10).
  7. Match mark joint hub, ball cage (8), constant velocity (CV) joint hub (9) and driveshaft.
  8. Remove joint hub.
  9. Remove balls from ball cage.
  10. Remove CV joint hub from driveshaft using a suitable drift.

    11. NOTE: Remove and discard sealing ring (6).

  11. Remove and discard snap ring (7).
  12. Remove ball cage from CV joint hub.
  13. Remove gaiter seal, gaiter and clamps from driveshaft.

    14. NOTE: Discard clamps.

  14. Using a suitable tool, release clamps (9a and 9b) and slide gaiter (9) along driveshaft (7).
  15. Match mark CV joint (10), joint hub (10a) and driveshaft (7).
  16. Remove CV joint and joint hub from driveshaft using a suitable drift.
  17. Remove gaiter and clamps from driveshaft.

    18. NOTE: Discard clamps.

  18. Remove and discard snap ring (7b).

    Installation

  1. Installation is the reverse of removal procedure except for the following:
  2. Clean CV joint, joint hub, ball cage, CV joint hub and balls and inspect for wear. Renew if necessary.

    3. WARNING: Only use cleaning agents and solvents in a well ventilated area.

  3. Insert balls into ball cage and grease with long-life grease (NLGI grade 2).
  4. Renew discarded sealing ring of joint hub.

    5. NOTE: Do not apply grease or oil to sealing ring otherwise gaiter seal may turn on joint hub.

  5. Pack gaiters and joint hubs with remaining long-life grease (NLGI grade 2).
  6. Renew discarded clamps.

    7. NOTE: Dimension "X" must be ≤ 2 mm otherwise leaks may occur at gaiter.

  7. Renew discarded snap rings.
  8. Apply grease to inboard driveshaft splines before installation.

    9. CAUTION: Take care when installing driveshaft into gearbox to not damage the snap ring.

    NOTE: Applying grease will keep the snap ring in place, aiding in installation.

  9. Check transmission oil level. Refer to procedure.
